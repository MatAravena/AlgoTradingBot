1. RSI + MACD (Mid-Term)
    Entry Signals
    Oversold Divergence

    RSI(14) makes a higher low while price makes a lower low, and MACD(12-26-9) histogram is rising.

    Signal-Line Crossover in Oversold Zone

    MACD line crosses above its 9-period signal when RSI(14) is below 30.

    Dual-RSI Confirmation + MACD Zero-Cross

    Fast RSI(7) crosses above RSI(14) and MACD line crosses above zero.

    Zero-Line Rejection

    MACD dips toward zero then bounces up, with RSI(14) recovering above 40.

    Multi-Timeframe Filter

    Daily RSI(14) exits oversold, weekly RSI already >50, and MACD(12-26-9) line crosses its signal.

    Exit Signals
    Signal-Line Cross Down near Overbought

    MACD line crosses below its 9-period signal when RSI(14) >70.

    Bearish Divergence

    Price makes a higher high, RSI(14) makes a lower high, and MACD histogram shows lower highs.

    Zero-Line Crossover Down

    MACD line falls below zero, often confirmed by RSI crossing down through 50.

    Fast-RSI Pullback + MACD Flop

    RSI(7) crosses below RSI(14) and MACD line crosses below its signal.

    ATR-Based Stop / Time Exit

    Exit if price moves against entry by 1×ATR(14) or after a fixed number of bars (e.g. 15–30 daily).

2. Bollinger Bands + EMA + ATR (Mid-Term)
    Entry Signals
    BB Squeeze Breakout

    BB(14, 2.0) narrow compression → price closes above upper band when EMA(20) is rising.

    EMA Pullback to Mid-Band

    Price retraces to BB mid-band (20 SMA) and bounces above EMA(20) with ATR(14) increasing.

    ATR-Confirmed Breakout

    Price breaks above BB upper band and ATR(14) spikes above its 14-bar average; EMA(9) > EMA(21).

    Dual-EMA Crossover + BB Filter

    EMA(9) crosses above EMA(21) within the bands, then a close above the upper BB(20, 2).

    Volatility Spike Entry

    ATR(20) >1.5× its 20-bar average and price sits above EMA(50) and BB(20, 2).

    Exit Signals
    Opposite-Band Touch

    Price hits the lower BB band (for longs) or upper band (for shorts).

    EMA Cross Down

    EMA(9) crosses below EMA(21) or EMA(20), confirmed by a close back inside BB.

    ATR-Based Trailing Stop

    Trail stop = entry price ± k × ATR(14) (commonly k=1–2).

    Band Re-Squeeze

    BB width contracts below a threshold while price falls below EMA(20).

    Time-Filter Exit

    Close after a set number of bars (e.g. 15–30) or when ATR(14) falls to <50% of its entry value.
